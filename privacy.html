Privacy Policy

Aligned with the United Nations Digital Public Goods (DPG) Standard

This open-source localized weather forecasting application is built as a climate-resilience tool to support smallholder farmers, research practitioners, and local communities. We value your absolute privacy and strictly practice Data Minimization in compliance with DPG Standard 4.

Zero Personal Tracking Notice: This application does not collect, log, or share any Personally Identifiable Information (PII). We do not collect, capture, or store your IP address, device IDs, or network configurations.

1. Information We Retrieve and Store

When you voluntarily choose to submit feedback via our evaluation form to help validate the accuracy of our forecasting engine, we only record the following non-identifiable, objective parameters in our MySQL database:

  • The user-entered custom location (or dynamic region coordinates text).
  • Outdoor activity status (Yes/No) and the specific activity type selected (e.g., Dry Palay, Laundry, Outdoor Event).
  • The approximate time and date of the activity.
  • Your subjective 0-to-10 rating of the application's forecast accuracy.
  • Your assessment of whether the forecast helped you plan or protect assets.
  • Your choice on which backend meteorological model (MET Norway or Pirate Weather) was more precise.
  • The timestamp of the submission.
  • Comment or suggested improvement for the app.

2. Anti-Spam & Rate Limiting

To keep this application lightweight and secure against server resource abuse without compromising your privacy, we implement a strictly local 5-minute wait timer for the feedback response form. This configuration relies entirely on temporary server-side session tokens or local cookies. It does not look at, write, or track your network IP address.

3. Third-Party API Data Requests

When fetching live hourly updates, the app queries third-party providers (MET Norway and Pirate Weather). These requests are made directly from our application server backend—not your browser—acting as a proxy. Your custom coordinate searches are sent to these providers anonymously to pull the correct localized forecast matrix.

4. Academic Research & Open Data Use

The anonymous metrics collected via the feedback form are utilized by affiliated academic research departments and community extension programs to run statistical error checks, improve the dual-model matching algorithm, and gauge local agricultural impact. Because the dataset contains entirely anonymous metrics, it is safe for open-science replication and open-source validation pipelines.
